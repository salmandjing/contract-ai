POWER PURCHASE AGREEMENT

This Power Purchase Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on January 15, 2024, between SolarTech Energy LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Seller"), and Metro Electric Utility Company, a public utility corporation organized under the laws of California ("Buyer").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Seller owns and operates a 50 MW solar photovoltaic generation facility located at 1234 Desert View Road, Riverside County, California ("Facility");

WHEREAS, Buyer desires to purchase renewable energy to meet its renewable portfolio standard obligations under California law;

WHEREAS, the parties wish to enter into a long-term agreement for the sale and purchase of electrical energy and environmental attributes;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. TERM AND COMMERCIAL OPERATION DATE

1.1 Term: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of twenty (20) years from the Commercial Operation Date.

1.2 Commercial Operation Date: The Commercial Operation Date is expected to occur on or before June 30, 2024. Seller shall provide sixty (60) days written notice of the expected Commercial Operation Date.

1.3 Extension: The term may be extended by mutual written agreement of the parties.

2. SALE AND PURCHASE OF ENERGY

2.1 Energy Sale: Seller agrees to sell and deliver, and Buyer agrees to purchase and receive, all electrical energy generated by the Facility during the term of this Agreement.

2.2 Delivery Point: The delivery point shall be at the point of interconnection with Buyer's transmission system at the Facility.

2.3 Scheduling: Seller shall provide day-ahead and hour-ahead generation forecasts in accordance with CAISO requirements.

3. PRICING AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 Energy Price: Buyer shall pay Seller $52.50 per megawatt-hour (MWh) for all energy delivered during the first ten (10) years of the term.

3.2 Price Escalation: Beginning in year eleven (11), the energy price shall escalate annually by two percent (2%).

3.3 Environmental Attributes: The price includes all renewable energy credits, green tags, and environmental attributes associated with the energy.

3.4 Payment Terms: Buyer shall pay all undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days of receipt of invoice.

4. PERFORMANCE GUARANTEES

4.1 Minimum Annual Generation: Seller guarantees minimum annual generation of 120,000 MWh, subject to force majeure events and scheduled maintenance.

4.2 Availability Guarantee: Seller guarantees the Facility will be available for generation at least 95% of the time, excluding force majeure and scheduled maintenance periods.

4.3 Liquidated Damages: If Seller fails to meet the minimum annual generation, Seller shall pay liquidated damages equal to the replacement cost of energy not delivered.

5. FORCE MAJEURE

5.1 Definition: Force majeure events include acts of God, natural disasters, wars, terrorism, labor disputes, and governmental actions beyond the reasonable control of the affected party.

5.2 Notice: The affected party shall provide prompt written notice of any force majeure event.

5.3 Mitigation: Each party shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of force majeure events.

6. DEFAULT AND TERMINATION

6.1 Events of Default: Material breach of this Agreement that remains uncured after thirty (30) days written notice shall constitute an event of default.

6.2 Remedies: Upon default, the non-defaulting party may terminate this Agreement and pursue all available legal remedies.

6.3 Survival: Certain provisions shall survive termination, including payment obligations and confidentiality requirements.

7. INSURANCE AND LIABILITY

7.1 Insurance Requirements: Seller shall maintain comprehensive general liability insurance of not less than $10,000,000 per occurrence.

7.2 Additional Insured: Buyer shall be named as an additional insured on Seller's liability policies.

7.3 Limitation of Liability: Neither party shall be liable for consequential, incidental, or punitive damages.

8. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

8.1 Permits and Approvals: Each party shall obtain and maintain all necessary permits, licenses, and approvals required for performance under this Agreement.

8.2 Environmental Compliance: Seller shall comply with all applicable environmental laws and regulations.

8.3 NERC Compliance: Both parties shall comply with all applicable NERC reliability standards.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Negotiation: The parties shall first attempt to resolve disputes through good faith negotiations.

9.2 Mediation: If negotiation fails, disputes shall be submitted to binding mediation.

9.3 Arbitration: Unresolved disputes after mediation shall be submitted to binding arbitration under AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules.

10. MISCELLANEOUS

10.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

10.2 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations and agreements.

10.3 Amendment: This Agreement may only be amended by written agreement signed by both parties.

10.4 Severability: If any provision is deemed invalid, the remainder of the Agreement shall remain in full force and effect.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.
