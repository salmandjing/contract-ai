RENEWABLE ENERGY POWER PURCHASE AGREEMENT

This Renewable Energy Power Purchase Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into as of March 1, 2024, by and between WindPower Generation Inc., a Texas corporation ("Seller"), and Regional Energy Cooperative, a cooperative corporation organized under Texas law ("Buyer").

ARTICLE I - DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 "Commercial Operation Date" means the date on which the Facility has achieved commercial operation as certified by an independent engineer.

1.2 "Contract Year" means each twelve (12) month period beginning on the Commercial Operation Date and each anniversary thereof.

1.3 "Facility" means the 75 MW wind generation facility located in Nolan County, Texas, as more particularly described in Exhibit A.

1.4 "Net Energy Output" means the electrical energy produced by the Facility, measured in MWh at the Delivery Point.

ARTICLE II - TERM AND CONDITIONS PRECEDENT

2.1 Term: This Agreement shall have a term of twenty-five (25) years commencing on the Commercial Operation Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with the terms hereof.

2.2 Commercial Operation Date: The Commercial Operation Date shall occur no later than December 31, 2024. Time is of the essence.

2.3 Conditions Precedent: This Agreement is subject to the following conditions precedent:
    (a) Seller obtaining all required permits and approvals;
    (b) Execution of an interconnection agreement with ERCOT;
    (c) Completion of construction financing arrangements;
    (d) Delivery of required insurance certificates.

ARTICLE III - SALE AND PURCHASE OBLIGATIONS

3.1 Sale and Purchase: Subject to the terms and conditions hereof, Seller shall sell and deliver to Buyer, and Buyer shall purchase and receive from Seller, one hundred percent (100%) of the Net Energy Output from the Facility.

3.2 Delivery Point: The Delivery Point shall be the high-voltage side of the step-up transformer at the Facility.

3.3 Title and Risk of Loss: Title to and risk of loss of the Net Energy Output shall transfer from Seller to Buyer at the Delivery Point.

3.4 Renewable Energy Credits: All renewable energy credits, green certificates, and environmental attributes generated by the Facility shall be transferred to Buyer at no additional cost.

ARTICLE IV - PRICING AND PAYMENT

4.1 Contract Price: Buyer shall pay Seller the following prices for Net Energy Output delivered:
    - Years 1-10: $48.75 per MWh
    - Years 11-20: $51.25 per MWh  
    - Years 21-25: $54.00 per MWh

4.2 Capacity Payments: In addition to energy payments, Buyer shall pay Seller a capacity payment of $3.50 per kW-month for the Contract Capacity of 75 MW.

4.3 Billing and Payment: Seller shall invoice Buyer monthly for all amounts due. Payment shall be made within twenty (20) days of receipt of invoice.

4.4 Late Payment: Overdue amounts shall bear interest at the prime rate plus two percent (2%) per annum.

ARTICLE V - OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Operations and Maintenance: Seller shall operate and maintain the Facility in accordance with Prudent Utility Practices and manufacturer recommendations.

5.2 Planned Outages: Seller shall coordinate all planned maintenance outages with Buyer with at least thirty (30) days advance notice.

5.3 Forced Outages: Seller shall notify Buyer immediately of any forced outages and provide estimated restoration times.

5.4 Performance Standards: Seller shall maintain the Facility to achieve:
    - Minimum annual capacity factor of 35%
    - Maximum annual forced outage rate of 5%
    - Availability factor of at least 97%

ARTICLE VI - PERFORMANCE GUARANTEES AND REMEDIES

6.1 Annual Energy Guarantee: Seller guarantees delivery of at least 230,000 MWh annually, adjusted for wind resource variations using the P90 wind assessment.

6.2 Shortfall Damages: If annual energy delivery falls below the guarantee, Seller shall pay damages equal to the cost of replacement energy at prevailing market rates.

6.3 Excess Energy: Energy delivered above 280,000 MWh annually shall be compensated at 90% of the Contract Price.

ARTICLE VII - CREDIT AND SECURITY

7.1 Credit Requirements: Seller shall maintain a credit rating of BBB- or higher from Standard & Poor's or provide alternative security.

7.2 Performance Security: Seller shall provide and maintain performance security in the amount of $5,000,000 throughout the term.

7.3 Step-Down Provisions: Performance security may be reduced to $2,500,000 after five (5) years of satisfactory performance.

ARTICLE VIII - INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION

8.1 Insurance Coverage: Seller shall maintain the following insurance:
    - Commercial general liability: $25,000,000 per occurrence
    - Property insurance: Full replacement cost of the Facility
    - Business interruption: 12 months of lost revenue
    - Environmental liability: $10,000,000

8.2 Additional Insured: Buyer shall be named as additional insured on all liability policies.

8.3 Mutual Indemnification: Each party shall indemnify the other against third-party claims arising from its negligent acts or omissions.

ARTICLE IX - EVENTS OF DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

9.1 Events of Default: The following shall constitute Events of Default:
    (a) Failure to make payment when due (after 10-day cure period)
    (b) Material breach of Agreement (after 30-day cure period)
    (c) Bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings
    (d) Abandonment of the Facility

9.2 Remedies: Upon Event of Default, the non-defaulting party may:
    (a) Terminate this Agreement
    (b) Seek specific performance
    (c) Pursue monetary damages
    (d) Draw on security instruments

ARTICLE X - FORCE MAJEURE

10.1 Force Majeure Events: Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures due to force majeure, including:
    - Natural disasters and severe weather
    - Acts of terrorism or war
    - Labor strikes or disputes
    - Governmental actions or changes in law
    - Grid emergencies or curtailments

10.2 Notification and Mitigation: The affected party shall promptly notify the other party and use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

ARTICLE XI - REGULATORY AND ENVIRONMENTAL MATTERS

11.1 Regulatory Compliance: Each party shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations.

11.2 Environmental Attributes: Seller warrants that the Facility qualifies as a renewable energy resource under applicable renewable portfolio standards.

11.3 Tax Credits: Seller shall be entitled to all federal and state tax credits associated with the Facility.

ARTICLE XII - DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 Informal Resolution: Disputes shall first be addressed through good faith negotiations between senior executives.

12.2 Mediation: Unresolved disputes shall be submitted to mediation administered by JAMS.

12.3 Arbitration: If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration under JAMS Comprehensive Arbitration Rules.

12.4 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by Texas law.

ARTICLE XIII - GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 Assignment: Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, except to affiliates or in connection with financing.

13.2 Confidentiality: Each party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other party's proprietary information.

13.3 Notices: All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified in Exhibit B.

13.4 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, including all exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

13.5 Amendment: This Agreement may only be amended by written instrument signed by both parties.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have caused this Agreement to be executed by their duly authorized representatives.
